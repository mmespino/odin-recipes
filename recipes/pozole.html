Pozole Recipe

Description

Pozole is a traditional Mexican dish. While there is red Pozole, I think it is better to make white Pozole and have the spicy sauce as a side which can be added to white Pozole and mixed to the individual's taste.

Ingredients (serves eight)

  • 2 to 4 pounds bone-in pork shoulder, chopped into large chunks.
  • 1 pound bone-in country-style pork ribs.
  • 1 pig trotter (optional), chopped into 6 pieces.
  • 1 onion (cut into four pieces)
  • 1 head of garlic.
  • 2 bay leaves.
  • 8 Mexican limes or key limes, quartered.
  • 8 Avocados, cubed.
  • Dried Mexican oregano.
  • 2 leaves of fresh Mexican oregano.

Cooking Instructions

  1. Put the pork, onion, head of garlic, and fresh oregano in a large pot and add enough water to cover it by about 2 inches.
  2. Bring to a boil, skimming off any foam that forms in the pot.
  3. Reduce the heat to maintain a steady simmer, cover, and cook until the pork is fork tender, about 90 minutes.
  4. Remove the pork pieces to a platter. Once cool enough to handle, separate the meat, discarding bones and skin.
  5. Add meat back to the stew.
  6. Drain canned hominy and add hominy plus Mexican oregano to the stew.
  7. Add salt to taste.
  8. Continue cooking at a simmer until flavors blend, for another hour.
  9. Serve in a deep bowl and use dried oregano, quartered limes, and cubed avocados for garnish to personal taste.
